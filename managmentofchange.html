Management of Change (MoC)

Standard Operating Procedure for technical and organizational modifications.

1. Initiation Phase

1.1 Submit Notification

The change initiator formally raises a Notification of Change describing the nature, purpose, and expected outcomes of the proposed modification.

1.2 Description of Change

A clear and complete description of the change is prepared, including the technical rationale, operational impact, and any relevant background information.

1.3 Pre-Approval to Proceed

The initiating department obtains preliminary authorization to continue with the assessment.

"This preapproval confirms that the change is valid, aligned with operational needs, and suitable to enter the evaluation stage."

Authorization Granted

2. Assessment Phase

2.1 Technical & Safety Impact

A multidisciplinary team assesses the technical implications, operational constraints, and Health, Safety, and Environmental (HSE) impacts of the proposed change.

2.2 Budget & Schedule

The initial cost and timeline required to implement the change are defined. This estimate provides management with the necessary inputs for the review.

Timeline & Cost Defined

2.3 Internal Review Meeting

Review Committee Evaluates:

  • Technical assessments
  • Safety considerations
  • Budget & Planning
Instruction to Proceed

3. Execution Path Decision

3.1 Determine Execution Method

A decision is taken on whether the change will be Executed Internally or Outsourced to a Contractor based on: Internal competencies, resource availability, cost, schedule, safety requirements, and technical complexity.

4. Execution Phase

4.1 Internal Execution (If "Yes")

4.1.1 Change Execution by Internal Team

The internal team carries out the approved work in accordance with safety, engineering, and operational standards.

4.1.2 Execution Follow-Up

Progress is monitored to ensure compliance with technical specifications and planned timelines. Any deviations, risks, or issues are reported and resolved promptly.

4.2 Contractor Execution (If "No")

4.2.1 Preparation of Scope

Responsible function prepares technical scope, RFQ documents & specs.

4.2.2 Tendering Process

Formal process ensuring fairness. Bidders invited, clarifications issued.

4.2.3 Proposal Review & TBE

Evaluation of technical compliance, safety & cost. TBE Report prepared.

4.2.4 Contractor Award & PO

Management approves bidder. Purchase Order (PO) issued.

4.2.5 Execution & Follow-Up

Contractor executes under supervision. Adherence to specs/safety checked.

5. Close-Out Phase

5.1 As-Built Documentation

Upon completion of the work, the responsible team compiles and reviews all as-built documents, including updated drawings, procedures, certifications, and inspection records.

5.2 Close-Out of Notification of Change

Once documentation is verified and requirements are met, the Notification of Change is formally closed. The modification is recorded in the appropriate databases and repositories for future reference, audits, and regulatory compliance.
